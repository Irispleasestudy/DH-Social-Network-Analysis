01 — Research Navigate

Propose and Questions

1.1 Research Propose

This study takes the narrative space of the Seven Mysterious Sects in "A Record of a Mortal's Journey to Immortality" as the object of analysis, using social network analysis methods to conduct a structured examination of the relationships among its characters. The study revolves around two objectives: first, to provide a topological description of the character relationship network of the Seven Mysterious Sects at the overall level, clarifying the network's connectivity structure, density level, and core–periphery distribution; second, based on individual characters' degree centrality, betweenness centrality, and clustering coefficient, to summarize the recurring structural role types in the network and assign them narrative-functional designations. Overall, this study does not intend to replace close reading of the text with quantitative analysis but rather aims to offer a verifiable, structural reference for the functional positioning of characters.

02 — Research Process

Data, Methods, and Analysis Pipeline

2.1 Data Source

The texts used in this study are from A Record of a Mortal's Journey to Immortality written by Wang Yu. Chapters specifically related to the narrative space of the 'Seven Mysterious Sects' were selected, specifically from Chapter 1 to Chapter 99. This period covers the main experiences of the novel's protagonist Han Li from entering the Seven Mysterious Sects to leaving this sect, representing the narrative section with the densest character relationships and the most complete sect structure of the Seven Mysterious Sects. The corpus can be downloaded at sudugu.org/128/txt.html#dir. The total word count of the text is approximately 190,000 words.

2.2 Analysis Steps

2.2.1 Determining Character Selection Criteria

The character selection in this study follows the following criteria: Inclusion criteria are (1) having a clear name or stable title in the text; (2) having an explicit interaction such as dialogue, conflict, or cooperation with at least one other character. Exclusion criteria are (1) characters who are only mentioned in the narration but never appear; (2) background characters who have neither name nor interaction, such as "a disciple" or "a passerby."

According to the above criteria, a total of 28 characters were selected, covering the sect leaders, elders, hall masters, inner disciples, outer disciples, and relevant personnel of the Seven Mysterious Sect.

2.2.2 Relationship Extraction and Encoding

This study primarily uses Python, supplemented by manual verification, to perform relationship extraction. First, Python is used to segment the text into sentences and perform named entity recognition, extracting a set of characters and calculating co-occurrence frequencies to generate a list of candidate relationships. Subsequently, project members conduct a preliminary manual verification. Ultimately, 87 valid relationship edges are obtained.

2.2.3 Analysis of Indicators

2.2.3.1 Problem 1 — Network Topology
Connectedness

Are all people in the network connected? Are people isolated or separated into isolated groups? — the overall accessibility and degree of fragmentation of the network.

Density Level

What is the actual number of connections as a percentage of the maximum possible? — the overall tightness and cohesion of the network. The higher the number, the more like a society of acquaintances.

Core–Periphery

Are there a small number of "core people" tightly connected to each other, while "periphery people" are only connected to the core? — an uneven distribution of power and resources.

Network computing with Python + NetworkX. Relational data is entered as an edge list and read by pandas. Connected structures are captured by connected_components. Density is calculated by the density function. The core–periphery distribution is divided into core layer (≥0.6), transition layer (0.2–0.6), and edge layer (<0.2) based on normalised degree centrality. Spring Layout was used for visualisation, and colour mapping was used to classify the above three layers.

2.2.3.2 Problem 2 — Individual Role Metrics
Degree Centrality

How many people does this person know? → Social activity level.

Betweenness Centrality

Is this person a "middleman"? → Information and resource control.

Clustering Coefficient

Do this person's friends also know each other? → In tight circles or loosely connected.

Based on the data from this document, the calculation of the three centrality measures was performed using the NetworkX library in Python. First, a network was constructed based on co-occurrence analysis of the original text: 20 characters served as nodes, and an edge was established whenever two characters appeared together in the same narrative unit, resulting in an undirected graph with 53 edges and a density of 0.279. Then, three core functions were applied: degree_centrality() calculates the proportion of connections a node has relative to the maximum possible, reflecting social activity; betweenness_centrality() computes the frequency with which a node lies on the shortest paths between other pairs, measuring control over information flow; and clustering() determines the ratio of actual to possible connections among a node's neighbors, indicating local cohesiveness. The results were exported as a table: Han Li has a degree centrality of 0.9474, a betweenness centrality of 0.2609, and a clustering coefficient of 0.0083; Doctor Mo has the highest betweenness at 0.4187; and Zhang Tie has the highest clustering coefficient at 0.0906. This process enables automated quantification from literary co-occurrence to network metrics.

Reproducibility note:
1. The Corpus of the novel saved as NOVEL TEXT.txt.
2. All of the analysis and charts in this article can be reproduced by running a script. The code is archived as CODE for future reference.

05 — Conclusions

Research Conclusions

Based on chapters 1–100 of A Record of a Mortal's Journey to Immortality, this study uses social network analysis to quantitatively and systematically analyse the relationship network of the characters in the Qixuanmen narrative space. Based on the above findings, four conclusions are drawn:

Conclusion 1
Protagonist Centralisation as a Structural Law
The core–periphery distribution of the relationship network of Qixuanmen characters is extremely different: the degree centrality of Han Li (0.95) is much higher than that of all other characters, and the whole network only forms a "star" topology with him as a single core. This structural feature is not a case unique to A Record of a Mortal's Journey to Immortality, but a quantitative reflection of the "protagonist-centered" narrative convention in the level of character relations in network literature. Different from the multi-centre and multi-threaded network of characters in classical literature, xianxia novels highly compress the complexity of the school world into a single-centre radiation network from the perspective of the protagonist; the transmission of information, resources, and conflicts are all based on the protagonist node as the only hub.

This conclusion has methodological value for the comparative study of xianxia literature: by calculating the ratio of the degree centrality of the protagonist nodes to the average value of the whole network (about 5.8 times in this study), one can quantify the degree of "centralising the protagonist" in different works and provide an operable index for horizontal comparison of genre literature.
Conclusion 2
Betweenness Centrality Reveals Implicit Narrative Power
The most valuable finding of this study is the following: in the narrative network of the Qixuan sect, the betweenness centrality of the person with the highest nominal power (Sect Leader Wang, bc = 0.057) was much lower than that of the guest elder (Doctor Mo, bc = 0.419) and the outer member (Third Uncle Han, bc = 0.327). This systematic misalignment of "structural power" and "nominal power" shows that the expression of power in xianxia narration is not single and linear; instead, it embeds a set of implicit power logic contrary to the formal power hierarchy through the narrative structure itself.

The high betweenness centrality of Doctor Mo and Third Uncle Han stems from their special "structural position" in narrative arrangement: the former is the only bridge between the devotion system and the core of the school management; the latter is the only introduction channel between the external world and the internal world of the Qixuan School. The existence of these two "structural holes" not only endows these two characters with structural power beyond their narrative proportion but also implies that xianxia narrative often uses key plot events to construct the power pattern artificially, giving specific functional roles — guides, devotees, hermits — irreplaceable narrative value.
Conclusion 3
Clustering Coefficient Reveals Inter-Layer Structural Isolation
In the Qixuanmen network, the clustering coefficient of most characters is close to zero, and the only node with a significantly high clustering coefficient in the whole network — Zhang Tie (cc = 0.091) — has exactly zero betweenness centrality. The reverse correspondence between the two shows that there is an obvious phenomenon of "inter-layer isolation" in the narrative network: between the characters deeply embedded in a certain narrative layer (such as the disciples' small circle) and the characters with cross-layer intermediary ability, two distinct modes of narrative function are constituted with little overlap between the two.

This conclusion shows that the narrative levels of the Qixuan school — the devotion level, the management level, the disciple level, and the outer door level — are relatively closed in structure: there is a certain self-consistency within each level, and communication between layers depends on a few "pivot nodes." This inter-level isolation structure is highly corresponding to the fine setting of the school system in xianxia novels (different ranks, regions, and practice systems), showing that the structural form of the narrative network itself carries the function of world-view construction.
Conclusion 4
Social Network Analysis as an Operational Quantitative Path
Traditional literary criticism's classification of character functions — such as Propp's functional classification and Greimas's role model — relies on researchers' subjective interpretation, which is difficult to avoid interpretation bias. Through the combined analysis of three quantitative indicators, this study identifies five types of character functions with internal consistency, and verifies the rationality of each type of narrative with evidence from the original plot. The advantage of this approach is that the classification is transparent and reproducible, and the coverage of the text is systematic. All valid nodes are included in the classification rather than relying on the researchers' selective interpretation of key paragraphs.

Of course, social network analysis has inherent limitations: it cannot capture the emotional texture of relationships (love, hostility, fear), and it is difficult to deal with dynamic changes in the nature of relationships (for example, relationships changing from cooperation to antagonism). In this study, co-occurrence frequency is used as relationship weight, which simplifies the complexity of narrative relationships to a certain extent. On this basis, future research can introduce directed graphs, emotional annotation, and time series analysis to track the evolution of relationship networks in different chapters.
